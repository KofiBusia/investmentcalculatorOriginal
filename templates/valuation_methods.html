Understanding Portfolio Valuation

Valuation methods estimate the worth of a company or portfolio based on different financial metrics. Each method provides a unique perspective on value, suitable for various scenarios.

Multiple Methods

Choose the Right Approach

Select a method based on the company’s context, such as market-based (CCA) or asset-based (NAV) approaches.

Data Accuracy

How to Use the Valuation Calculator

  1. Select Valuation Method

    Choose a method from the dropdown (e.g., CCA, NAV, Market Capitalization).

  2. Enter Financial Data

    Provide the required inputs based on the selected method. Use financial statements or market data.

  3. Calculate and Review

    Click “Calculate” to estimate the value. Review the result to understand the company’s worth.

Key Valuation Methods

  • CCA: Uses P/E ratio and earnings to compare with similar companies.
  • NAV: Subtracts liabilities from assets for asset-based valuation.
  • Market Capitalization: Multiplies share price by shares outstanding.
  • EV: Combines market cap, debt, and cash for a comprehensive value.
  • Replacement Cost: Estimates cost to recreate assets, adjusted if needed.
  • Risk-Adjusted Return: Calculates Sharpe Ratio to assess risk-adjusted performance.

To calculate accurate valuations, source reliable financial data for each method. Here’s how to find the required inputs for global and Ghanaian companies:

  1. Comparable Company Analysis (CCA)

    Requires P/E ratio and earnings:

    • P/E Ratio: Find on Yahoo Finance under “Summary” or “Statistics” for similar companies.
    • Earnings: Check the income statement on Yahoo Finance or gse.com.gh for net income.
  2. Net Asset Value (NAV)

    Requires total assets and liabilities:

    • Assets and Liabilities: Find on the balance sheet via Yahoo Finance, gse.com.gh, or annualreportsghana.com.
  3. Market Capitalization

    Requires share price and shares outstanding:

    • Share Price: Check current price on gse.com.gh or Yahoo Finance.
    • Shares Outstanding: Find in financial statements or company reports.
  4. Enterprise Value (EV)

    Requires market cap, debt, and cash:

    • Market Cap: Calculate as share price × shares outstanding.
    • Debt and Cash: Find on the balance sheet under liabilities and cash equivalents.
  5. Replacement Cost

    Requires tangible and intangible assets:

    • Tangible and Intangible Assets: Check balance sheet for property, equipment, patents, or goodwill.
    • Adjustment Factor: Optional; use 1 unless specific adjustments are needed.
  6. Risk-Adjusted Return

    Requires portfolio return, risk-free rate, beta, and market return:

    • Portfolio Return: Estimate based on historical or expected returns.
    • Risk-Free Rate: Use Ghana treasury bill rates or U.S. treasury rates.
    • Beta: Find on Yahoo Finance under “Statistics.”
    • Market Return: Use historical GSE index returns or global market indices.
  7. Tips
    • Verify data from multiple sources (e.g., Yahoo Finance, gse.com.gh, company reports).
    • For Ghanaian companies, check gse.com.gh for financial statements and announcements.
    • Use analyst estimates for projections (Yahoo Finance “Analysis” tab).

Examples:

  • Access Bank Ghana (ABG): P/E = 12, Earnings = GHS 217.5M (CCA); Assets = GHS 2B, Liabilities = GHS 800M (NAV).
  • JPMorgan Chase (JPM): Share Price = $150, Shares = 2.9B (Market Cap); Market Cap = $435B, Debt = $300B, Cash = $500B (EV).
