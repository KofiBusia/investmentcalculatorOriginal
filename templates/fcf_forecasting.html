FCF Formula

FCF = [Revenue × EBIT Margin × (1 - Tax Rate)] + (Revenue × D&A %) - (Revenue × CapEx %) - ΔNWC

Free Cash Flow (FCF) represents the cash available after operating expenses, taxes, and investments in capital and working capital, ideal for valuation or investment decisions.

Understanding Free Cash Flow Forecasting

FCF forecasting estimates a company’s cash flow available for investors, critical for valuation, investment planning, or assessing financial health.

Low FCF

e.g., Negative or Low FCF

May indicate high reinvestment needs, operational inefficiencies, or growth-phase spending.

High FCF

e.g., Strong Positive FCF

Reflects efficient operations, financial flexibility, and potential for dividends or reinvestment.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Gather Financial Data

    Collect the following inputs from reliable sources:

    • Revenue (Year 1): Find total sales or revenue from the income statement in the company’s annual report or 10-K filing. For example, a company might report GHS 1,000,000,000 in revenue.
    • Revenue Growth Rate (%): Estimate from management guidance in annual reports, investor presentations, or analyst forecasts (e.g., Bloomberg, Yahoo Finance). Use historical growth rates if unavailable:
      Growth Rate = [(Current Year Revenue / Previous Year Revenue) - 1] × 100
      Typical range: 5%–15% for stable companies.
    • EBIT Margin (%): Calculate from the income statement:
      EBIT Margin = (EBIT / Revenue) × 100
      EBIT (Earnings Before Interest and Taxes) is often listed as operating income. Typical range: 10%–20%.
    • Tax Rate (%): Use the effective tax rate from the income statement:
      Effective Tax Rate = (Income Tax Expense / Pre-Tax Income) × 100
      If unavailable, use statutory rates (e.g., 25% in Ghana) or industry averages (20%–30%).
    • D&A as % of Revenue (%): Find Depreciation and Amortization (D&A) in the cash flow statement and divide by revenue:
      D&A % = (D&A / Revenue) × 100
      Typical range: 3%–8%.
    • CapEx as % of Revenue (%): Find capital expenditures in the cash flow statement (under investing activities) and divide by revenue:
      CapEx % = (Capital Expenditures / Revenue) × 100
      Typical range: 5%–10%.
    • NWC as % of Revenue (%): Calculate Net Working Capital (NWC = Current Assets - Current Liabilities) from the balance sheet, then divide by revenue:
      NWC % = (NWC / Revenue) × 100
      Typical range: -1,000% to 20% for financial institutions, 5%–20% for other industries.

    Sources: Company filings (10-K, 10-Q), annual reports, Bloomberg, Yahoo Finance, or industry reports.

  2. Input Year 1 Data

    Enter the starting revenue and assumptions for Year 1 using the values gathered. Refer to tooltips for typical ranges if unsure (e.g., 10%–20% for EBIT margin).

  3. Add Forecast Years

    Click “Add Another Year” to project up to 5 years. Revenue for subsequent years is automatically calculated using the growth rate from the previous year.

  4. Validate Inputs

    Ensure all fields are filled with valid numbers. Errors are highlighted in red with feedback (e.g., “Please enter a valid number”).

  5. Calculate FCF

    Click “Calculate” to generate the FCF forecast. The results table shows detailed projections per year, including revenue, EBIT, D&A, CapEx, ΔNWC, and FCF.

  6. Analyze Results

    Use the forecasted FCF for Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation in the DCF Calculator, investment decisions, or to compare with industry peers. Check the interpretation for insights (e.g., negative FCF may indicate reinvestment needs).

  7. Refine Assumptions

    Adjust inputs (e.g., lower growth rate for conservative estimates) to test scenarios (optimistic, pessimistic) and recalculate to see how FCF changes.

Tip: Cross-check assumptions with management guidance or analyst estimates from sources like Bloomberg or Yahoo Finance for accuracy.

To forecast FCF accurately, gather data from reliable sources:

  • Financial Statements: Find revenue, EBIT, D&A, CapEx, and NWC components (current assets, liabilities) in income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements.
  • Management Guidance: Check annual reports, investor presentations, or earnings calls for revenue growth and margin projections.
  • Industry Benchmarks: Use industry averages for EBIT margin (10%–20%), D&A (3%–8%), CapEx (5%–10%), and NWC (-1,000% to 20% for financial institutions, 5%–20% for others) from sources like Bloomberg, Yahoo Finance, or analyst reports.
  • Historical Data: Analyze past financials from company filings (e.g., 10-K, 10-Q) to estimate realistic growth rates and percentages.
