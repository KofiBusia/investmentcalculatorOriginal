The Basics

What is a Stock Pitch?

Research a Company

Deep-dive into a publicly listed company — its financials, industry, competitive position, and growth prospects.

Calculate its Value

Use professional valuation models — DCF, DDM, Intrinsic Value, Target Price — to determine what the stock is truly worth.

Make a Recommendation

Argue confidently whether investors should BUY, SELL, or HOLD the stock — backed by data.

Your Opening Statement

"I think that [Company Name] is a BUY / SELL / HOLD because [qualitative reason] and our valuation shows the stock is [overvalued / undervalued / fairly priced] at its current price of [GHc X]."

This 15-second pitch combines qualitative insight with quantitative valuation — both are required.

The 6-Step Framework

How to Build Your Stock Pitch

Follow these six steps in order. Each one feeds into the next — skip none.

1

Economic Analysis

Macro View

Before analysing any company, understand the broader economic environment. A great company in a struggling economy can still be a bad investment.

Ghana Macro Factors

  • GDP growth rate — is the economy expanding or contracting?
  • Inflation rate — how does it affect company costs and revenues?
  • Interest rates (Bank of Ghana policy rate) — affects borrowing costs
  • Exchange rate (GHc/USD) — critical for import-heavy companies
  • Government spending and fiscal policy

Key Questions to Answer

  • Is Ghana's economy growing fast enough to support earnings growth?
  • Are interest rates rising? (Bad for indebted companies)
  • Is inflation hurting consumer purchasing power?
  • Are there regulatory changes affecting the sector?
  • What external global factors (oil price, commodities) matter?

WHERE TO FIND THIS DATA

Bank of Ghana (bog.gov.gh) · Ghana Statistical Service (statsghana.gov.gh) · IMF World Economic Outlook · World Bank Data Portal

2

Industry Analysis

Sector View

Even excellent management cannot overcome a structurally broken industry. Understand the competitive landscape your company operates in.

Porter's 5 Forces (Simplified)

  • 1. Competition: How fierce is rivalry between existing companies?
  • 2. New entrants: How easy is it for new competitors to enter?
  • 3. Substitutes: Can customers switch to an alternative product?
  • 4. Suppliers: Do suppliers have power to raise prices?
  • 5. Buyers: Do customers have power to demand lower prices?

Industry Metrics to Research

  • Is the industry growing, stable, or declining?
  • What is the average profit margin in this sector?
  • Who are the top 3 companies? What market share does your company have?
  • Is there government regulation that limits competition?
  • What are the key risks unique to this industry?

Company Analysis

Deep Dive

This is the heart of your pitch. Know your company inside out — its business model, financials, management, and competitive advantage.

Business Overview

  • What does the company do?
  • How does it make money?
  • Who are its customers?
  • Where does it operate?
  • How long has it been listed?

Financial Metrics

  • Revenue (3-year trend)
  • Net profit margin
  • Earnings Per Share (EPS)
  • Return on Equity (ROE)
  • Debt-to-Equity ratio
  • Dividend per share

SWOT Analysis

  • S Strengths (moat, brand)
  • W Weaknesses (gaps, debt)
  • O Opportunities (growth)
  • T Threats (competition, FX)

KEY DATA TO COLLECT FROM ANNUAL REPORT

Market Cap

Shares × Price

Net Debt

Total Debt − Cash

Enterprise Value

Mkt Cap + Net Debt

EPS

Net Profit ÷ Shares

Valuation

Use InvestIQ Calculators

This is where InvestIQ does the heavy lifting for you. Use our professional calculators to compute intrinsic value — then compare to the current market price.

The Core Valuation Logic

If Intrinsic Value > Current Market Price → Stock is undervalued → Consider BUY
If Intrinsic Value < Current Market Price → Stock is overvalued → Consider SELL
If Intrinsic Value ≈ Current Market Price → Stock is fairly valued → Consider HOLD

DCF Valuation

Best for: companies with predictable free cash flows. Enter revenue growth, margins, WACC, and terminal growth to get intrinsic value per share.

You need: FCF, growth rate, WACC →

Intrinsic Value (Graham)

Benjamin Graham's formula. Simple and powerful for any stock. Gives you a margin of safety — how much the stock is discounted from its true value.

You need: EPS, growth rate →

Target Price

Analyst-style price target using Forward EPS × Target P/E multiple. Compare target price to current price to identify upside or downside.

You need: EPS forecast, P/E multiple →

Bank Stock Valuation

If pitching a bank stock (GCB, CAL, Absa, Stanchart, etc.), use this DDM-based model specifically designed for financial institutions.

You need: DPS, ROE, cost of equity →

Dividend Valuation (DDM)

Gordon Growth Model — values stocks that pay regular dividends. Ideal for mature, dividend-paying GSE companies.

You need: DPS, dividend growth, cost of equity →

Cost of Equity (CAPM)

Calculate the required return on equity — the discount rate you'll use in DDM and DCF models. CAPM + DDM blended approach.

You need: risk-free rate, beta, market return →

FCFF Calculator

Free Cash Flow to the Firm — calculates the cash available to all capital providers. Used in enterprise valuation (EV) models.

You need: EBIT, taxes, capex, depreciation →

FCFE Calculator

Free Cash Flow to Equity — the cash left for equity holders after debt payments. Used for equity-specific valuation.

You need: net income, capex, debt changes →

Beta Calculator

Measure a stock's systematic risk relative to the GSE market. Beta > 1 means higher risk/reward. Used in CAPM cost of equity.

You need: stock returns vs market returns →

Pro Tip — Use Multiple Models

Don't rely on one model alone. Run at least two or three, then take an average (weighted or equal). Show the judges you understand the range of possible values. Example:

DCF Value

GHc 4.20

Graham Value

GHc 3.85

Average (Target)

GHc 4.03

Recommendation

Buy · Sell · Hold

Based on your full analysis, make a clear and confident recommendation. Be decisive — a vague "it depends" is not a recommendation.

BUY

  • Intrinsic value significantly above market price
  • Strong earnings growth outlook
  • Competitive advantage is durable
  • Macroeconomic tailwinds

SELL

  • Stock is significantly overvalued
  • Earnings are declining or under pressure
  • Competitive moat is eroding
  • Major macro or regulatory headwinds

HOLD

  • Stock is fairly valued at current price
  • Steady but unexciting growth outlook
  • No strong catalyst to drive price up or down
  • Good dividend yield compensates for flat price

Your Recommendation Slide Should Include:

Current Price Target Price Upside / Downside % Time Horizon (e.g. 12 months) Key Risks to Watch BUY / SELL / HOLD verdict

6

Conclusion

Tie it All Together

End strong. Your conclusion is your last chance to convince the judges. Summarise succinctly — don't introduce new information.

CONCLUSION STRUCTURE

1

Restate your thesis: "We recommended [Company] as a BUY based on our analysis showing it is undervalued by X%."

2

Top 3 reasons: Summarise your strongest arguments — macro tailwind, strong earnings, undervalued by DCF.

3

Acknowledge key risks: "The main risk to our thesis is X. If Y happens, our recommendation changes to Z."

4

Close with conviction: End confidently — thank the judges, restate your verdict, invite questions.

Competition Details

Everything you need to know about the YIN Stock Pitch Competition

Teams

  • Groups of 1 to 5 people maximum
  • Open to persons who completed school within the last 3 years
  • Any field of study — finance, engineering, arts, science, all welcome

Presentation Format

  • 10 minutes presentation + 5 minutes Q&A with judges
  • 10 slides MAX (excluding title and appendices)
  • Print 5 copies of slides to hand to judges
  • Bring your laptop with the presentation ready

Stock Selection Rules

  • Must be a stock listed on the Ghana Stock Exchange (GSE)
  • Must be a common or preferred stock (not bonds)
  • Stock must have had trading activity within the last 12 months

How You're Judged

  • Quality and depth of your financial analysis
  • Strength of your arguments and recommendation
  • Overall flow and clarity of your presentation
  • Accuracy in answering judges' questions
