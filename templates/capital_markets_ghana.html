SEC Ghana — Capital Market Licenses

Securities Industry Act, 2016 (Act 929) · Securities Industry (Licensing) Regulations, 2003 (LI 1728)

Fund Management Company

GHS 3,000 App
Act 929, Sec. 108–120 · LI 1728

Manages investment funds (mutual funds, hedge funds, unit trusts) on behalf of investors on a discretionary basis.

Minimum Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 500,000
Incorporated as a limited liability company in Ghana
Minimum 2 licensed fund managers (individual licensees) as directors/key personnel
Separate custodian for client assets (cannot self-custody)
Fit-and-proper assessment of directors and key personnel
Professional Indemnity Insurance in place
AML/CFT compliance programme (FIC registration required)

Application Steps:

1

Incorporate company with Registrar General's Department (RGD). Obtain Certificate of Incorporation + Commencement.

2

Draft Investment Policy Statement, Offering Document (if managing a fund), and Client Agreement templates.

3

Open dedicated client omnibus account with an approved custodian bank.

4

Register with FIC as a reporting entity under AML Act 1044. Appoint AML/CFT Compliance Officer.

5

Obtain individual Fund Manager licenses for all key personnel managing assets (see Individual License below).

6

Complete SEC Application Form CF1 (Corporate) and pay non-refundable application fee of GHS 3,000.

7

Submit certified documents: Memorandum & Articles, RGD certificates, audited financials (or opening balance sheet), CVs of directors, fit-and-proper forms, business plan, compliance manual.

8

SEC conducts due diligence review (typically 60–90 working days). SEC may request additional information or conduct site inspection.

9

Upon approval, pay annual licensing fee of GHS 6,000 and receive Fund Management License (valid 1 year, renewable).

Annual Renewal: GHS 6,000 · Due 31st January each year · Late renewal attracts penalty.

Investment Adviser / Wealth Manager

GHS 2,000 App
Act 929, Sec. 82–95 · LI 1728

Provides investment advisory services to clients — asset allocation, portfolio recommendations, financial planning — without necessarily having discretionary management authority.

Minimum Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 150,000
At least 1 licensed Investment Adviser (individual) on staff
Written Advisory Agreement templates for all clients
Disclosure document on conflicts of interest and fee structures
Compliance manual aligned with SEC regulations

Application Steps:

1

Incorporate entity at RGD with "investment advisory" as primary object in M&A.

2

Prepare business plan, compliance manual, client advisory agreement templates, and fee disclosure document.

3

Register key personnel with SEC for individual Investment Adviser licenses.

4

Complete SEC Form CF1, pay GHS 2,000 application fee, and submit full documentation package.

5

Respond to any SEC queries within 15 working days. Await approval and pay annual fee of GHS 4,000.

Annual Renewal: GHS 4,000

Broker-Dealer (Stockbroker)

GHS 5,000 App
Act 929, Sec. 60–80 · GSE Membership Rules

Executes buy and sell orders for securities on the Ghana Stock Exchange (GSE) or OTC markets on behalf of clients and/or as principal.

Minimum Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 2,000,000 (Broker-Dealer with dealing licence)
Minimum 2 licensed Dealing Officers (individual SEC licenses)
Fidelity bond / surety bond to cover client funds
GSE Trading Membership (applied separately from GSE)
Client asset segregation — dedicated client omnibus account
Robust back-office settlement systems (T+2 compliance)
Compliance officer and AML/CFT programme

Application Steps:

1

Incorporate. Ensure M&A objects include "dealing in securities" and "brokerage."

2

Apply for SEC corporate Broker-Dealer license (Form CF1) — GHS 5,000 fee.

3

Separately apply to GSE for Trading Membership. Pay GSE entry fee + annual subscription.

4

Obtain Fidelity Bond and provide SEC with proof of surety.

5

Register all dealing officers under individual SEC Dealing Officer licenses.

6

SEC inspection of back-office systems and compliance infrastructure before final approval.

7

Upon approval pay annual license fee GHS 10,000 and commence operations.

Annual Renewal: GHS 10,000 · GSE Annual Subscription: separate (contact GSE)

Corporate Trustee

GHS 4,000 App
Act 929 · Trustee Incorporation Act · Act 766 (for pension trustees)

Holds assets in trust for beneficiaries of collective investment schemes, pension funds, or bond issuances. Acts as independent overseer of fund assets.

Minimum Requirements (SEC Corporate Trustee):

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 1,000,000
Deed of Trust (standard SEC-approved format)
Custodian infrastructure — own or contracted to approved bank
At least 2 qualified personnel with relevant trust/legal qualifications
Surety/fidelity bond covering trustee obligations
Trust deed for each scheme/fund must be registered with SEC

Steps:

1

Incorporate. M&A must include "trustee" and "custodial" services as primary objects.

2

Apply SEC Form CF1 for Corporate Trustee license. Pay GHS 4,000 application fee.

3

Submit draft Deed of Trust, compliance manual, organisational chart, CVs of key staff.

4

Demonstrate settlement and custody infrastructure to SEC (site inspection may be required).

5

Receive approval and pay annual fee GHS 8,000.

Annual Renewal: GHS 8,000

Collective Investment Scheme (CIS) / Mutual Fund

GHS 5,000 App
Act 929, Sec. 121–175 · Securities Industry (CIS) Rules

A pooled investment vehicle that collects money from investors and invests in a diversified portfolio. Includes mutual funds, unit trusts, fixed-income funds, money market funds.

Minimum Requirements:

Fund Manager must already hold a SEC Fund Management License
Corporate Trustee appointed (holds assets for investors)
Approved Custodian (licensed bank) to hold fund assets
Minimum initial fund size: GHS 500,000 at launch
Audited financial statements from fund manager parent company
Prospectus / Offering Document — SEC-prescribed format
Fund accounting and NAV computation procedures documented

CIS Registration Steps:

1

Prepare Prospectus/Offering Document covering fund objectives, investment strategy, risk factors, fees, and redemption terms.

2

Execute Trust Deed between Fund Manager and Corporate Trustee.

3

Execute Custody Agreement with licensed custodian bank.

4

Submit CIS registration application to SEC with all executed agreements and Offering Document. Pay GHS 5,000.

5

SEC reviews and may issue comments. Revise Offering Document and resubmit. Process typically 45–90 working days.

6

Receive SEC No-Objection Letter. Seed the fund to minimum GHS 500,000. File confirmation with SEC.

7

Commence marketing and investor onboarding. NAV must be computed and published per SEC rules (at minimum weekly).

Ongoing obligations: Quarterly reports to SEC · Annual audited accounts · Immediate disclosure of material events

Market Operator / Stock Exchange

GHS 20,000 App
Act 929, Sec. 30–55

Operates a securities exchange or alternative trading system. Highest-tier capital markets license — requires SEC's highest scrutiny.

Key Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 10,000,000
Robust trading rules, listing standards, and membership criteria
Central depository linkage (CSD — Ghana CSD)
Trading and settlement infrastructure meeting T+2 standard
Dispute resolution mechanism for market participants
Market surveillance and manipulation prevention systems

Ghana Alternative Exchange (GAX)

Ghana Stock Exchange (GSE) · Securities Industry Act, 2016 (Act 929) · GAX Rules

The Ghana Alternative Exchange (GAX) is the second board of the Ghana Stock Exchange, specifically designed to provide SMEs, startups, and high-growth companies access to equity capital with lighter listing requirements than the Main Board. GAX is ideal for companies too early-stage or too small for the Main GSE Board but needing growth capital through public equity.

GAX Listing Requirements

Capital & Size

Minimum issued share capital: GHS 250,000 (post-IPO)
No minimum net assets requirement (unlike Main Board)
Minimum float (public shareholding): 15% of issued shares

Operating History

Minimum 1 year of operating history (vs. 3 years on Main Board)
Startups with innovative business models may apply with as little as 6 months history at GSE discretion

Governance

Minimum 3 directors; at least 1 independent non-executive director
Audit committee required (minimum 3 members)
Mandatory appointment of a Nominated Adviser (NOMAD)

Financial Reporting

Audited financial statements for at least 1 year (IFRS / IFRS for SMEs)
Unaudited half-year results within 2 months of period end
Full-year audited results within 4 months of year-end

Special Startup & Innovation Provisions

Why GAX was designed for startups:

  • Shorter operating history requirement (1 year vs. 3 years)
  • Lower minimum capital (GHS 250,000 vs. GHS 1,000,000+ on Main Board)
  • NOMAD model — you have a sponsor who guides you through compliance
  • No profitability requirement — loss-making growth companies can list
  • Proportional listing fees — lower than Main Board
  • Technology and innovation companies receive fast-track consideration

Key legal basis (Act 929):

The Securities Industry Act, 2016 (Act 929) empowers the GSE to establish alternative listing segments. The SEC has approved the GAX Rules under Act 929 as the regulatory framework for alternative market listings. All GAX-listed companies remain subject to continuous disclosure obligations under Act 929, Sections 176–210.

GAX Listing Process — Step by Step

1

Appoint a NOMAD (Nominated Adviser)

Select a GSE-approved NOMAD firm. They will guide you through the entire listing process, conduct due diligence, and act as your ongoing compliance adviser post-listing.

2

Appoint Listing Advisers

Legal counsel (transaction attorneys), reporting accountants (auditors), broker-dealer (for share marketing), and registrar (for shareholder management).

3

Conduct Due Diligence & Restructuring

Legal, financial, and tax due diligence. Restructure corporate governance to meet GAX standards (board composition, audit committee, etc.).

4

Prepare Prospectus / Information Memorandum

Draft the Prospectus (or Information Memorandum for private placements) covering business description, financial history, risk factors, use of funds, and financial projections.

5

Submit to SEC for Review

File the Prospectus with SEC Ghana for vetting under Act 929, Sec. 180. SEC has 40 working days to review and clear the Prospectus.

6

Apply to GSE for GAX Listing

Submit GAX Listing Application to Ghana Stock Exchange with SEC clearance, completed Prospectus, and all supporting documents. Pay GSE application fee.

7

GSE Listing Committee Review

GSE Listing Committee evaluates the application. May issue questions. Approval typically takes 10–20 working days after complete submission.

8

Public Offer / Book Build

Launch public offer (IPO) or private placement. Your broker-dealer markets shares to investors. Subscription period typically 2–4 weeks.

9

Admission to Trading

After successful offer and allotment, shares are admitted to trading on GAX. CSD registers shareholders. First day of trading.

10

Ongoing Continuous Disclosure

Immediately disclose material events, publish half-year and full-year results, maintain minimum float, file annual returns. NOMAD remains your ongoing compliance guide.

Bank of Ghana — Banking & Financial Institution Licenses

Banks and Specialised Deposit-Taking Institutions (BSDI) Act, 2016 (Act 930) · BOG Directives

Universal Bank

GHS 400M Min Capital
Act 930, Sec. 1–60 · BOG Banking Directives

Full commercial banking services: deposits, lending, FX, trade finance, investment banking, and all other financial services. Highest regulatory tier.

Key Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 400,000,000 (GHS 400 million)
Minimum capital adequacy ratio (CAR): 13%
At least 5 qualified directors; majority must be Ghanaians
Minimum liquidity ratio: 30% of total deposits
Primary reserve requirement: 14% of total deposits held at BOG
Robust core banking system and IT infrastructure
AML/CFT framework per FIC Act 1044 — Compliance Officer mandatory
At least 1 independent non-executive director with banking expertise
Deposit Protection membership (Ghana Deposit Protection Corporation)

Application Steps:

1

Secure promoter shareholders with fit-and-proper status. Foreign banks: obtain home-country regulator No Objection Letter.

2

Submit Letter of Intent (LOI) to BOG with business plan, ownership structure, and proof of capital availability.

3

BOG grants Approval-in-Principle (AIP). Incorporate at RGD. Open escrow account — deposit full minimum capital GHS 400M.

4

Submit full license application: M&A, full business plan (5 years), IT system specs, AML/CFT programme, compliance manual, CVs/fit-and-proper forms for all directors and key management.

5

BOG conducts due diligence and on-site inspection of proposed premises, IT systems, and organisational readiness.

6

BOG issues banking license. Bank formally commences operations.

Application Fee to BOG: GHS 50,000 (non-refundable) · Annual Supervisory Fee: set by BOG based on total assets

Savings & Loans Company

GHS 15M Min Capital
Act 930 · BOG S&L Guidelines

Takes deposits from the public and provides credit — similar to banks but with limited branching and foreign exchange powers. Popular vehicle for specialist lenders.

Key Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 15,000,000
At least 5 directors with relevant financial experience
Capital adequacy ratio: minimum 10%
Primary reserve: 12% of deposits at BOG
Deposit-taking licence required (cannot take deposits without formal approval)
AML/CFT compliance programme and FIC registration
Core banking system with BOG-approved standards

Application Steps:

1

Prepare a detailed business plan (5 years), target market analysis, capital sourcing evidence.

2

Submit LOI with evidence of GHS 15M minimum capital to BOG Banking Supervision Department.

3

Receive BOG AIP. Incorporate at RGD. Pay-up minimum capital in escrow account at BOG-approved bank.

4

Submit formal application with all documentation. BOG conducts fit-and-proper on directors, key management.

5

BOG site inspection of premises and IT systems. Receive final license and commence operations.

Application Fee: GHS 20,000 · Annual supervisory levy based on assets

Finance House

GHS 7.5M Min Capital
Act 930 · BOG Finance House Guidelines

Non-bank financial institution providing consumer finance, hire purchase, leasing, and structured finance. Cannot take demand deposits from the public.

Key Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 7,500,000
Funding from wholesale (institutional) sources; limited retail deposit-taking
At least 3 experienced directors
Specialised lending policies for product types (hire purchase, leasing, etc.)
Credit risk management framework

Steps:

1

Submit LOI to BOG with business plan, promoter profiles, and source of capital.

2

Incorporate at RGD. Fund escrow account with GHS 7.5M minimum capital.

3

Submit formal application with all documents. Fit-and-proper checks. Site visit. License issued.

Rural & Community Bank (RCB)

GHS 1M Min Capital
Act 930 · BOG RCB Guidelines · ARB Apex Bank Act

Community-owned banks serving rural and peri-urban populations. Affiliated with the ARB Apex Bank for supervision and clearing services.

Key Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 1,000,000
Majority community ownership — shares held primarily by community members
At least 5 board members with community representation
Affiliation with ARB Apex Bank mandatory
Operates in defined geographical community
SACCO/credit union experience in at least one board member desirable

Steps:

1

Conduct community mobilisation — sell shares to community members. Minimum of 500 shareholders from the target community.

2

Raise minimum GHS 1,000,000 paid-up capital from share subscriptions.

3

Apply to ARB Apex Bank for membership and to BOG for RCB license.

4

BOG and ARB Apex Bank conduct joint review and site inspection. License granted.

Microcredit Company / Money Lender

GHS 100K Min Capital
Act 930 · Moneylenders Act · LI 2362

Provides small loans to individuals and micro-enterprises. Cannot take deposits from the public. Lighter regulatory burden — good entry point for consumer lenders.

Key Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 100,000
Maximum interest rate caps apply (BOG sets from time to time)
Loan products must be clearly documented
Consumer protection disclosures mandatory (true cost of credit)
At least 2 directors with relevant experience
AML/CFT compliance even at this tier (FIC registration)

Steps:

1

Incorporate at RGD. Deposit GHS 100,000 minimum capital.

2

Submit application to BOG Other Financial Institutions Department. Pay GHS 1,000 application fee.

3

Receive Microcredit license. Annual renewal GHS 500.

Common Requirements Across All BOG-Regulated Entities

Registration with Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA) — TIN required
Registration with Social Security and National Insurance Trust (SSNIT)
Registration with Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC) under AML Act 1044
Appointment of a licensed external auditor (must be ICAG member)
Compliance with Consumer Recourse Mechanism — mandatory complaints handling procedure
Annual submission of audited financial statements to BOG within 4 months of year-end
Quarterly regulatory returns submission to BOG
Compliance with BOG's Data Protection requirements and cybersecurity directives

BOG Disclaimer

All minimum capital requirements are subject to change by BOG Directives. The GHS 400M Universal Bank minimum was set in 2018. Contact BOG Banking Supervision Department (bog.gov.gh) or call +233 (0)302 666174 for current requirements.

NPRA — National Pensions Regulatory Authority

National Pensions Act, 2008 (Act 766) as amended by National Pensions (Amendment) Act, 2014 (Act 883) · NPRA Regulations, 2011 (LI 1990)

Ghana operates a three-tier pension system under Act 766. Understanding the tiers is essential before applying for any NPRA license:

Tier 1 — Basic National Social Security

Mandatory. Managed by SSNIT. 13% employer + 5.5% employee contribution. NPRA does not license private managers for Tier 1.

Tier 2 — Occupational Scheme

Mandatory. 5% employee contribution. Must be managed by a licensed Tier 2 Fund Manager. Employer must establish or join an approved occupational scheme.

Tier 3 — Voluntary Provident Fund

Voluntary. Additional voluntary savings by employees/self-employed. Managed by licensed Tier 3 Fund Managers. Tax-exempt contributions (up to limits).

Corporate Trustee (Pension)

GHS 3,000 App
Act 766, Sec. 109–130 · LI 1990

Holds pension fund assets in trust for scheme members. Acts as independent custodian and oversees fund administration. Required for all Tier 2 and 3 schemes.

Minimum Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 500,000
At least 3 directors, including 1 legal professional and 1 finance professional
Trust infrastructure: separate trust accounts for each scheme
Surety bond covering minimum 12 months' trustee obligations
Deed of Trust for each scheme registered with NPRA

Steps:

1

Incorporate at RGD with "trustee" as primary object.

2

Prepare Deed of Trust template, compliance manual, surety bond, and director CVs/fit-and-proper forms.

3

Apply to NPRA — pay GHS 3,000 non-refundable fee. Submit full documentation.

4

NPRA review (60–90 days). Upon approval, pay annual license fee GHS 5,000.

Pension Fund Manager (Tier 2 & 3)

GHS 5,000 App
Act 766, Sec. 131–160 · LI 1990 · NPRA Investment Guidelines

Manages pension fund assets — invests contributions in compliance with NPRA investment guidelines. Must have both an NPRA licence AND a SEC Fund Management licence.

Minimum Requirements:

Minimum paid-up capital: GHS 2,000,000
Must also hold SEC Fund Management Corporate License (dual licensing)
At least 3 licensed individual Fund Managers (SEC) on staff
Separate pension fund account for each scheme managed
Compliance with NPRA Investment Policy Guidelines (asset class limits)
Monthly NAV reporting to NPRA on each scheme

NPRA Investment Limits (Fund Managers must comply):

Pension Custodian

GHS 5,000 App
Act 766, Sec. 161–175

Safekeeps pension fund assets. Must be a licensed bank or financial institution. Separate from the Fund Manager (independence principle).

Requirements:

Must be a BOG-licensed bank or depository institution
Minimum capital: aligns with the applicant's existing BOG license category
Separate custody accounts for each pension scheme
Cannot be the same entity as the Fund Manager (independence)
Sub-custody capabilities for foreign assets if managing foreign allocations

Establishing a Pension Scheme (Employers)

GHS 500 App
Act 766, Sec. 50–90

Every employer with 5+ employees is required to establish or participate in an approved Tier 2 occupational pension scheme.

Employer Obligations:

Register with NPRA and SSNIT within 3 months of commencing business
Deduct 5% of basic salary from employees (Tier 2)
Remit contributions to licensed Tier 2 Fund Manager monthly (by 14th of following month)
Provide employees with pension benefit statements annually
Late remittance penalty: 3% per month on overdue contributions

Scheme Registration Steps:

1

Select a licensed Tier 2 Fund Manager and licensed Trustee. Execute Participation Agreement.

2

Execute Trust Deed between Trustee and employer, and Scheme Rules document.

3

Submit Scheme Registration Form to NPRA with Trust Deed, Scheme Rules, list of participating employees. Pay GHS 500 registration fee.

4

NPRA issues Certificate of Registration. Begin contribution remittance monthly.

NPRA Disclaimer

Investment limits and contribution rates may be updated by NPRA guidelines. Verify current requirements at npra.gov.gh or call NPRA on +233 (0)302 913540. Pension fund managers are jointly regulated by both NPRA and SEC Ghana.

Important Notes on Fees

  • All fees are subject to annual revision by each regulator and may change without prior notice.
  • Application fees are generally non-refundable even if the application is rejected.
  • BOG annual supervisory levies are calculated on total assets and billed separately after licensing.
  • Late annual renewal attracts penalty surcharges — typically 10–30% of annual fee per regulator.
  • Additional costs include RGD incorporation fees, GRA TIN registration, FIC registration, and professional adviser fees.
  • Always obtain a current fee schedule directly from the regulator before budgeting.
