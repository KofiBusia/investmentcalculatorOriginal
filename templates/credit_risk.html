Calculate Credit Risk Metrics

Enter financial data for 3 to 5 years to calculate key credit risk metrics for a bank. All monetary values in Ghanaian Cedi (GHS). Example: Use data for Access Bank Ghana (174 million shares).

Credit Risk Formulas

1. Altman Z-Score: Z = 1.2 × (Working Capital / Total Assets) + 1.4 × (Retained Earnings / Total Assets) + 3.3 × (EBIT / Total Assets) + 0.6 × (Market Value of Equity / Total Liabilities) + 1.0 × (Operating Income / Total Assets)

2. PCL Ratio: |Net Impairment Loss| / Loans and Advances to Customers

3. Net Interest Margin (NIM): (Interest Income - Interest Expense) / Earning Assets

4. Efficiency Ratio: Non-Interest Expenses / Operating Income

5. Leverage Ratio: Total Shareholders’ Equity / Total Assets

6. Debt to Assets Ratio: Total Liabilities / Total Assets

Where Working Capital is current assets minus current liabilities, Earning Assets include loans, advances, investments, and cash, and Non-Interest Expenses include personnel, depreciation, and other expenses.

Understanding Credit Risk Metrics

Credit risk metrics assess a bank's financial stability and exposure to loan defaults, crucial for evaluating lending risk and solvency.

Low Credit Risk

Z-Score > 2.99, Low PCL, High NIM

High Z-Score, low PCL Ratio, and strong NIM indicate robust financial health and low default risk.

High Credit Risk

Z-Score < 1.81, High PCL, Low NIM

Low Z-Score, high PCL Ratio, or declining NIM suggest potential financial distress or high default risk.

How to Use the Credit Risk Calculator

  1. Select Number of Years

    Choose 3, 4, or 5 years for credit risk analysis in the "Select Number of Years" section.

  2. Input Annual Financial Data

    For each selected year, enter the following in GHS (find on gse.com.gh, Yahoo Finance, or annual reports):

    • Cash and Cash Equivalents: From balance sheet, under assets.
    • Non-Pledged Trading Assets: From balance sheet, under assets.
    • Loans and Advances to Customers: From balance sheet, under assets.
    • Advances to Banks: From balance sheet, under assets.
    • Other Assets: From balance sheet, under assets.
    • Deposits from Banks & Other FIs: From balance sheet, under liabilities.
    • Deposits from Customers: From balance sheet, under liabilities.
    • Current Tax Liabilities: From balance sheet, under liabilities.
    • Other Liabilities and Provisions: From balance sheet, under liabilities.
    • Retained Earnings: From balance sheet, under equity.
    • EBIT: From income statement, operating profit before interest and taxes.
    • Operating Income: From income statement, total operating revenue.
    • Total Assets: From balance sheet, total assets.
    • Total Liabilities: From balance sheet, total liabilities.
    • Market Value of Equity: For past years, use P/E Ratio × Profit (income statement); for current year, use market cap.
    • Net Impairment Loss: From income statement, impairment on loans/financial assets.
    • Interest Income: From income statement.
    • Interest Expense: From income statement.
    • Investment Securities: From balance sheet, under assets.
    • Personnel Expenses: From income statement.
    • Depreciation and Amortisation: From income statement.
    • Other Expenses: From income statement.
    • Total Shareholders’ Equity: From balance sheet, under equity.
  3. Enter Additional Parameters

    Provide the Start Year and Valuation Year for context.

  4. Calculate Credit Risk Metrics

    Click "Calculate" to compute the six credit risk metrics for each year.

  5. Review Results

    The results section displays:

    • Metric Results: Altman Z-Score, PCL Ratio, NIM, Efficiency Ratio, Leverage Ratio, Debt to Assets Ratio for each year.
    • Interpretation: Analysis of credit risk based on Z-Score thresholds and trends in other ratios.
    • Trend Table: A table showing metrics across years to identify stability or deterioration.
  6. Reset and Recalculate

    Use the "Clear" button to reset inputs or "Calculate Another" in the results section.

Download Guide Watch Tutorial

To calculate credit risk metrics, you need accurate financial data. Here's how to find the required information for global and Ghanaian banks:

  1. Balance Sheet Data

    Find in the Statement of Financial Position:

    • Cash and Cash Equivalents, Non-Pledged Trading Assets, Loans and Advances to Customers, Advances to Banks, Other Assets, Deposits from Banks & Other FIs, Deposits from Customers, Current Tax Liabilities, Other Liabilities, Retained Earnings, Total Assets, Total Liabilities, Total Shareholders’ Equity: From balance sheet (Yahoo Finance "Financials" > "Balance Sheet" or gse.com.gh).
  2. Income Statement Data

    Find in the Statement of Comprehensive Income:

    • EBIT, Operating Income, Net Impairment Loss, Interest Income, Interest Expense, Personnel Expenses, Depreciation and Amortisation, Other Expenses: From income statement (Yahoo Finance "Financials" > "Income Statement" or gse.com.gh).
  3. Market Value of Equity

    Calculate as P/E Ratio × Profit for past years (income statement) or use market cap for current year (gse.com.gh or Yahoo Finance "Summary").

  4. Investment Securities

    From balance sheet, under assets (Yahoo Finance or gse.com.gh).

  5. Tips
    • Verify data from multiple sources for accuracy.
    • For Ghanaian banks, check gse.com.gh for financial statements.
    • Use historical data for past years and analyst estimates for projections (Yahoo Finance "Analysis" tab).
