Methodology

DDM (Dividend Discount Model): Uses a two-stage model with a high-growth period followed by a terminal value calculated via the Gordon Growth Model: Dividend / (Discount Rate - Terminal Growth Rate).

DCF (Discounted Cash Flow): Projects Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE) over a high-growth period, discounted to present value, plus a terminal value discounted using the same rate.

P/E (Price-to-Earnings): Projects future EPS based on growth rate and applies a P/E multiple to estimate future stock price, discounted to present value.

How to Use the Multi-Method Stock Valuation Calculator

Follow these steps to estimate a stock's intrinsic value using multiple valuation methods.

  1. Select Currency

    Choose the currency for all monetary inputs and outputs (e.g., USD, GHS).

  2. Select Weighting Scenario

    Choose Conservative, Balanced, or Growth to weight the valuation methods (DDM, DCF, P/E).

  3. Enter General Parameters

    Provide the current stock price, high-growth years, growth rates, and discount rate. Use sources like financial websites or company reports.

  4. Enter DDM Parameters

    Input base and sensitivity dividends per share for the Dividend Discount Model.

  5. Enter DCF Parameters

    Provide the current Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE) per share for the Discounted Cash Flow model.

  6. Enter P/E Parameters

    Input current EPS, EPS growth rate, P/E multiple, and projection years for the Price-to-Earnings model.

  7. Calculate and Review

    Click “Calculate” to compute the weighted average intrinsic value. Review the table, chart, and sensitivity analysis to assess the stock’s valuation.
